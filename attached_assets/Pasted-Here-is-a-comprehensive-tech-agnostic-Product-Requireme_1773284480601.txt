> **Project: Build the "Celljevity Longevity Operating System"**
> **Product Vision:**
> Build a highly secure, scalable, and proactive "Longevity Operating System" that elevates the clinic to a Cleveland Clinic standard. This platform will transition patient management from a reactive, one-off treatment model to a proactive, lifelong relationship (The "Longevity Loop").
> 
> 
> **Tech Stack Guidelines:**
> Please choose the most robust, secure, and modern full-stack technologies suitable for healthcare applications (e.g., React/Next.js, Node.js/Python, and a relational database like PostgreSQL). The architecture must support strict data separation and secure document handling.
> ---
> 
> 
> ### 1. User Roles & Access Control (RBAC)
> 
> 
> The system must implement Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) from day one to ensure HIPAA compliance.
> * **Patient:** Can only access their own "MyCelljevity" portal. Can view their timeline, upload medical history, securely message staff, view approved quotes/invoices, and track biomarker progress.
> * **Care Coordinator (Admin/Ops):** Manages the CRM, scheduling, logistics, non-medical intake workflows, and billing/invoicing. Cannot alter medical protocols.
> 
> 
> * **Medical Provider (Doctor/Specialist):** Has access to clinical data. Reviews uploaded labs/documents, uses the Protocol Builder to prescribe treatments, and tracks patient outcomes.
> 
> 
> * **Super Admin:** System configuration, managing the Service Catalog (pricing/products), and user access management.
> 
> 
> ---
> 
> 
> ### 2. Core Platform Modules
> 
> 
> 
> **Module A: Patient Layer ("MyCelljevity" Portal)** * **Secure Login & Dashboard:** A Cleveland Clinic-style dashboard showing "My Journey", upcoming appointments, and action items.
> * **Document Vault:** Secure upload and storage for doctor's letters, past diagnoses, and lab results.
> * **Progress & Biomarker Tracker:** Visual representation of diagnostic results over time (e.g., biological age, telomere length).
> 
> 
> 
> **Module B: Journey Engine (Business Logic)** * **Intake Workflow:** Digital forms capturing medical history, goals, and terms/conditions sign-offs.
> * **Protocol Builder:** A tool for medical staff to construct personalized treatment plans (selecting NK Cells, Prometheus, Exosomes, etc.) based on diagnostics.
> * **Automated Reminder System:** Logic that triggers proactive follow-ups based on treatment timelines (e.g., "It has been 6 months, time for repeat diagnostics").
> 
> 
> 
> **Module C: Admin / Ops Layer (Backend)** * **Patient CRM:** Centralized view of all patients, their current journey phase (Acquisition -> Intake -> Diagnostics -> Planning -> Treatment -> Follow-up).
> * **Quote & Invoice Generator:** Dynamically pulls from the "Service Catalog" based on the doctor's Protocol. Converts prices dynamically, allows editable descriptions, and exports clean PDFs (hiding UI elements).
> * **Multi-Provider Scheduler:** Drag-and-drop calendar to coordinate patient travel, clinic rooms, and specific biological asset preparation.
> 
> 
> 
> **Module D: Service Catalog** * A centralized database of treatments (NK Cell Therapy, Prometheus Cells, Exosomes, Diagnostics, etc.) with associated base prices and descriptions.
> 
> 
> ---
> 
> 
> ### 3. HIPAA & Security Requirements
> 
> 
> Because this handles Protected Health Information (PHI), the application MUST strictly adhere to the following:
> * **Data Encryption:** All data must be encrypted in transit (HTTPS/TLS) and at rest (database-level encryption).
> * **Audit Trails:** Every action (viewing a patient record, modifying a quote, downloading a lab result) must be logged with a timestamp and user ID.
> * **Secure File Storage:** Uploaded documents (biopsies, lab results) must NOT be stored with public URLs. They must require authenticated, time-limited signed URLs to access.
> * **Session Management:** Implement secure, HTTP-only cookies or strict JWTs with automatic timeout/logout after periods of inactivity.
> * **No Third-Party Leakage:** Do not send PHI to external non-compliant APIs (e.g., standard public AI endpoints or analytics trackers) without explicit configuration.
> 
>
